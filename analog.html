Analog in Software Studies: A Lexicon: A Debriefing

In Matthew Fuller's book Software Studies: A Lexicon, in the "Analog" chapter, he delves into the history of analog technology, its shift into "digital" modes, and the subsequent refocus of analog technology beyond this era of digitalization (and how these computing modes have been consistently relevant in the periphery of the technological world). Much of these technologies' origins have been completely intertwined with historical events - notably analog technology that was weaponized for events such as World War II and the Vietnam War - and with this boom in technology (along with the severity of its stakes), like most things in the world, this analog (and slowly digitalizing technology) began to be commercialized and commodified. While many of these technologies were indeed clunky, they began building complex systems that interrelated within and with one another in an irreversible way, combining other modes of academic study such as electrical engineering, physics, and even focusing (such as in areas of commerce) on art.
