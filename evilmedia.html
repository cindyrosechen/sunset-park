E V I L  M E D I A

Enhance Queue Formation

In Evil Media by Matthew Fuller and Andrew Goffey, in the "Algorithms" section of the work, Enhance Queue Formation emphasizes the entrenchment of the idea of organized, consecutive process -- not only in our own lives but in the way societies structure themselves. From governmental organization to behavorial norms and process, "queueing" embodies the concepts of resource allocation, management, individual and community access, and even cognitive decision making and function. Fuller and Goffey use the Vladimir Sorokin's example of the Soviet queue in order to illustrate this concept at its fundamental level, in which Sorokin details the process and management that allows resources to be distributed on a mass level, and how such a commonplace / known method of functioning has becoming collectively ingrained in these people's (and our!) lives. From tangible spatial queueing to abstract knowledge, these "queues" neatly structure daily functioning.

Fuller and Goffey note that this is usually inherently generative, "yielding something other than itself." (Fuller, Goffey 70) Supermarkets exemplify a successful form of queue formation -- people go in without food, walk through meticulously planned isles, and walk out with food. The authors note that when this form of generative queueing is somewhat self-aware, it allows for positive forms of expression, as well as critical room for improvement. Thus, the due process of queueing "has been raised to the level of a surrogate for culture", and during these times, participation in the process is essential to being a proactive, "cultured", orderly member of society. Sometimes, slight individual deviance from this process can be humorous, pleasurable or even useful (i.e. car traffic and "waiting line models" can be jostled in order to speed up traffic flow). (70) In comparison, "neoliberal accounts", where capitalist-driven entities frame freedom of the market as inherently liberating, is pseudo-freedom marketed as good, whereas successful queue formation is structure aimed to positively organize. In the end, Fuller and Goffey note that it is not the existence of queue formation that is positive, but the precise and skillful management of it.
